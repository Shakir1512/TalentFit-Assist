# Fairness & Non-Discrimination Policy v1.0

## Purpose
This policy ensures that AI-assisted screening decisions are made fairly, transparently, and in compliance with all applicable employment laws and company values. The system assists humans in decision-making but never makes hiring decisions autonomously.

## Core Principles

### 1. Non-Discrimination
- Screening MUST be based solely on job-related qualifications, skills, experience, and abilities
- NO consideration of protected attributes or characteristics:
  - Age (birth date, graduation year, "digital native", "millennial", etc.)
  - Gender, gender identity, or sexual orientation
  - Race, color, ethnicity, or national origin
  - Religion or spiritual beliefs
  - Disability status (physical, mental, cognitive)
  - Military/veteran status
  - Marital/family status
  - Genetic information
  - Any other protected class

### 2. Evidence-Based Evaluation
- All screening scores must be justified with concrete evidence from submitted documents
- Evidence must reference specific skills, years of experience, or demonstrated abilities
- Inferences about capabilities based on demographics are prohibited
- Ambiguity in candidate qualifications must be clearly flagged for human review

### 3. Transparency
- Every candidate receives a clear scoring breakdown
- All evidence citations must include source document and location
- Interview focus areas must be grounded in job requirements, not assumptions
- Adverse actions (low scores) must include gap explanations

### 4. Human Decision Authority
- The system provides scoring and explanations, not recommendations
- Final hiring decisions remain with human recruiters and hiring managers
- System users cannot override or circumvent fairness guardrails
- Exception decisions require audit trail and manager approval

## Prohibited Practices

### Do NOT:
- Use education institution prestige as a scoring factor
- Consider candidate appearance, photo, or personal presentation
- Make assumptions based on name, pronunciation, or perceived origin
- Penalize employment gaps without business justification
- Use "culture fit" as a screening factor (grounds for unconscious bias)
- Infer protected attributes from resume information
- Weight factors differently based on candidate demographics

### Examples of Violations:
❌ "Stanford degree → better candidate" (prestige bias)
❌ "5-year gap → assumes caregiving duties" (family status inference)
❌ "Name sounds foreign → less American experience" (national origin bias)
❌ "Military service → leadership potential" (stereotyping)
❌ "Young graduate → tech-savvy" (age assumption)

## Required Practices

### DO:
✅ Compare candidates using identical scoring criteria
✅ Evaluate skills against job requirements only
✅ Document all scoring factors with evidence
✅ Flag ambiguous or unclear candidate information
✅ Report any system anomalies or potential bias
✅ Ensure candidate privacy and data security
✅ Maintain audit trails of all screening activities

### Examples of Compliant Screening:
✅ "JD requires 5+ years Python; candidate has 7 years → score 0.9"
✅ "Experience gap noted; unclear if related to domain shift or job search"
✅ "5 years in e-commerce, 2 in retail tech = 7 years relevant domain experience"
✅ "Skills match: has 4/5 must-haves; missing Kubernetes (nice-to-have)"

## Data Protection

- NO personal information retained beyond 30 days unless required by law
- Protected attributes are stripped at ingestion (one-time permanent removal)
- Even system administrators cannot access removed data
- All uploads encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256)
- Candidate data isolated by recruiter (no cross-org data leakage)

## Monitoring & Compliance

- Weekly bias detection scans on scoring patterns
- Monthly reports on score distributions by role
- Quarterly fairness audits with legal review
- Any suspicious patterns trigger immediate investigation
- Violations documented and reported to Compliance

## Accountability

- All system users must acknowledge this policy annually
- Violations may result in access revocation or employment action
- Candidates have right to request their screening explanation
- Legal holds and discovery processes respected automatically
